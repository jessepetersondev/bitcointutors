Lesson 8: Keeping Your Bitcoin Safe

Learn important safety tips to protect your digital money!

Keeping your Bitcoin safe is super important! Just like you wouldn't leave your allowance money on the sidewalk, you need to protect your Bitcoin with strong passwords and safe storage.

Key Points:

  • Never share your wallet keys or passwords with anyone
  • Use strong passwords that are hard to guess
  • Consider using a hardware wallet for extra security
  • Always double-check addresses before sending Bitcoin

The most important rule of Bitcoin safety is: never share your private keys or wallet passwords with anyone! Not your friends, not people online, not even your favorite teacher. The only exception might be your parents if they're helping you learn about Bitcoin.

Here are some important safety tips:

  • Use strong passwords: Don't use simple passwords like "bitcoin123" or your birthday. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols that would be hard for someone to guess.
  • Write down your recovery phrase: Most Bitcoin wallets give you a list of 12 or 24 words when you first set them up. This is called a "recovery phrase" and it's super important! Write it down on paper and keep it somewhere very safe.
  • Double-check addresses: Before sending Bitcoin to someone, always check the address carefully. If you send Bitcoin to the wrong address, you can't get it back!
  • Be careful of scams: If someone online promises to double your Bitcoin or give you free Bitcoin, it's probably a scam. Remember: if something sounds too good to be true, it probably is!

For extra security, some people use special devices called "hardware wallets" that look like small USB sticks. These keep your Bitcoin keys offline where hackers can't reach them, which makes them very secure.
